Open the one that fits your project and you start from a ready-made template instead of a blank canvas. Surveys can live in more than one suite when the work overlaps.

Step 1

Build your survey

Start from a validated template or a blank canvas. Add Likert, multiple-choice, open-ended, matrix, and ranking questions. Brand the cover, configure logic, and let AI review your wording before you launch.

01

Validated templates

Each one ships with the items, anchors, and recommended sample sizes you'd use in a peer-reviewed instrument.

02

AI question review

Catches double-barreled, leading, and missing-anchor problems and proposes specific rewrites.

03

Branching and skip logic

Conditional paths, eligibility screening, and follow-up questions tied to prior answers.

04

Branding and theming

Custom colors, logo, and a thank-you message that match your project or institution.

Step 2

Collect responses your way

Eight distribution methods, all organized in one hub. Live counts, partial-save, and close conditions. Every response is timestamped, audit-logged, and tagged with the channel it came from.

01

Web Link

A clean public URL anyone can use. Optional access password.

02

Email Invitation

Unique tokenized invitations with reminders, completion tracking, and re-send.

03

Conversational Mode

Respondents chat with an AI that maps their natural-language replies to the structured answer. Append ?chat=1 to the link.

04

Pulse Schedule

Schedule weekly, monthly, or quarterly waves. Each wave is auto-tagged for Pre/Post and Test-Retest analytics.

05

Slack and Teams

Post the survey-take card directly into a Slack channel or Microsoft Teams webhook. No OAuth, just paste the URL.

06

QR Code

A printable code that drops respondents straight into your survey. PNG and SVG downloads.

07

Website Embed

One-line iframe snippet for a course site, intranet page, or landing page.

08

Social Media

Pre-filled share to LinkedIn, X, and Facebook. Editable message, one click.

Step 3

Analyze with reliability checks

Cronbach's alpha, KMO, item-total correlations, and missing-data patterns are computed automatically. AI summaries explain what to trust and what to investigate.

Reliability

Per-scale internal consistency

Alpha, split-half, and confidence intervals for every scale, with method-aware caveats.

Items

Per-item flags

Item-total correlations and "alpha if deleted" projections so you see which items pull their weight.

Quality

Sample-size and missing-data warnings

ReliCheck flags estimates from small samples and tells you whether missing data is meaningful.
