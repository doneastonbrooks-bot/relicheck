Methodology

How ReliCheck computes its statistics

A reference for the methods behind every reliability number, item flag, and quality warning ReliCheck shows in your dashboard. Designed for researchers, evaluators, and instructors who need to defend the numbers they share.

Last updated May 2026 · Reading time: 12 min · Audience: research, evaluation, HR

Scope and conventions

This document describes the statistical methods ReliCheck applies to multi-item scale data collected through its survey platform. The intent is to give a researcher, evaluator, or instructor enough detail to reproduce, defend, or critique any number that appears in a ReliCheck report.

Throughout, n refers to the number of complete-case respondents on a given scale, k to the number of items, and item subscripts run 1 through k. Default rounding is two decimals for reliability statistics and three decimals for correlations.

Convention

Reliability statistics are computed only when at least three valid items and at least 30 complete-case respondents are available. Below those thresholds, ReliCheck displays the count instead of an estimate, with a sample-size warning.

Likert coding and reverse-scoring

Likert items are stored with their displayed labels and a numeric code. ReliCheck preserves the author's original anchors, applies forward coding by default, and offers per-item reverse-scoring during analysis. Reverse-scored items are flagged in every output so a reader can verify the direction of effect.

For a five-point scale coded 1 through 5, a reverse-scored item x becomes 6 − x. ReliCheck never silently flips coding. The flip is visible in the item table, the methods appendix of every export, and the audit log.

Cronbach's alpha

Cronbach's alpha is reported as the standard internal-consistency estimate for unidimensional Likert scales. ReliCheck computes alpha from the item covariance matrix using the standard formula:

α = (k / (k − 1)) × (1 − Σ var(item) / var(total))

The denominator var(total) is the variance of the row-summed scale score across complete-case respondents. The numerator sums each item's variance independently.

Reporting thresholds

ReliCheck flags scales with α < 0.70 as weak and α < 0.60 as unreliable. These cutoffs are conventions, not laws, and are explained alongside the number in every report so a reader sees both the value and the interpretation in context.

Confidence intervals

A 95% confidence interval is reported using the Feldt-Woodruff approximation, which assumes complete data and an interval-level scale. When either assumption is violated by the data, ReliCheck adds a method note rather than dropping the interval.

Split-half reliability

Split-half reliability is reported as a complement to alpha when the scale has at least four items. Items are split by odd/even index and by random partition; ReliCheck reports both, and the larger of the two divergence flags is shown.

The Spearman-Brown correction is applied to the half-test correlation to estimate full-test reliability. When the two halves disagree by more than 0.10, ReliCheck surfaces a halves disagree warning, which usually points to a multidimensional structure or a single dominant item.

KMO sampling adequacy

The Kaiser-Meyer-Olkin measure of sampling adequacy is computed at both the overall and per-item level. Overall KMO is reported as a single number; per-item KMO is shown in the item table next to each row.

Conventional cutoffs (Kaiser, 1974): KMO < 0.50 unacceptable, 0.50-0.59 miserable, 0.60-0.69 mediocre, 0.70-0.79 middling, 0.80-0.89 meritorious, ≥ 0.90 marvelous. ReliCheck shows the value, the band, and a one-sentence interpretation.

Item-total correlations

Two flavors are reported per item: the uncorrected correlation between the item and the row sum (which includes the item itself), and the corrected correlation between the item and the row sum minus the item. The corrected version is the one used for diagnostic flagging.

Items with a corrected item-total correlation below 0.20 are flagged as weak contributors and shown alongside an "alpha if deleted" projection so the analyst can see what the scale would look like without them.

Missing-data handling

ReliCheck reports unit non-response and item non-response separately. Reliability statistics are computed on complete cases by default, with a configurable pairwise-deletion option for KMO and inter-item correlations only.

Imputation is never applied to reliability estimation. When the analyst opts into imputation for downstream reporting, the method (mean, median, multiple imputation) is recorded in the methods appendix and surfaced as a banner on the relevant outputs.

Primary references

The methods above follow conventional psychometric practice as documented in Cronbach (1951), Spearman-Brown (1910), Kaiser (1974), Nunnally and Bernstein (1994), and Feldt & Woodruff (1989). Each output in ReliCheck links to the relevant reference for further reading.
