What reviewers look for

Evaluation evidence has to stand up to people who were not in the room.

Accreditors, funders, board members, and program reviewers do not know your faculty, your students, or your context. They have standards. They read survey-based evidence with a small set of questions in mind. Five of those questions account for most of the friction.

Reliable measurement

Reviewer expects

Evidence that the survey items used to support your claims are internally consistent and not capturing noise.

ReliCheck provides

Cronbach's alpha and McDonald's omega per scale with confidence intervals, plus item-if-deleted analyses that flag the items pulling reliability down.

Adequate response and inclusion

Reviewer expects

Response rate context, completion-quality flags, and confidence that the responders reflect the population the conclusions apply to.

ReliCheck provides

Per-survey completion stats, response-quality scoring (straight-lining, duplicate vectors, short open-ended answers), and subgroup composition checks against expected proportions.

Disaggregated by relevant subgroups

Reviewer expects

Outcomes broken out by program, demographic group, or instructional modality, with privacy protections for small groups.

ReliCheck provides

Subgroup analyses with effect sizes against the overall mean, automatic k-anonymity suppression for groups below the privacy floor, and configurable equity-gap flags that surface material subgroup differences.

Tied to stated outcomes

Reviewer expects

A clear line from survey items to learning outcomes, program goals, or strategic priorities, not a list of items that drift from the mission.

ReliCheck provides

Items mapped to constructs and outcomes inside the builder. Reports group results by outcome, so the evidence answers "did we move what we said we would move?" instead of "what did people respond to?"

Multi-year continuity and trend

Reviewer expects

Comparable results across waves and across years, with measurement that stays consistent so a change in scores reflects a change in the program.

ReliCheck provides

Pulse and wave tagging at the response level, year-over-year stability checks, and pre/post analyses that surface whether a change is real organizational movement or measurement drift.
