ReliCheck Intelligence · Before you collect

Build a survey that stays focused on the decision you need to make.

Five small assists help you write items that match the question, group them into constructs that hold together, and pull in a validated scale when one already exists. The assists are deliberately small, so you can stop at any point and still ship a focused survey.

Authoring tools

Five assists, used in any order.

✎

Item Audit

Catch the wording problems that quietly hurt your data.

Double-barreled, leading, ambiguous, and biased phrasing get flagged as you type, with a rewrite suggestion that keeps the scale anchors and sentiment intact. The original is always one click away.

Lives in the Questions builder.

⋈

Construct Map

Group your items into the ideas they actually measure.

Give the builder a list of items and Construct Map proposes two to six constructs, assigns every item to one, and writes a one-sentence rationale per group. Review in a preview modal, rename constructs inline, and apply the assignments only where the construct field was blank.

Lives in the Questions builder toolbar.

◎

Purpose Alignment

Keep the survey honest to the decision it is supposed to inform.

State the decision in plain language. Each item gets tagged as core, supporting, tangential, or off-topic; underrepresented aspects of the purpose are named, and specific replacement items are proposed for one-click insertion.

Lives at the top of the Questions builder.

✦

Item Drafter

Start from a topic, not a blank page.

Type a topic (team psychological safety, classroom engagement, customer trust) and pick how many items you want. Each draft is tagged with a construct and a reverse-score flag, ready to edit before it lands in the Questions tab.

Lives in the Questions builder toolbar.

⚖

Validated Scale Match

Use an instrument someone has already validated, whenever one fits.

Describe what you're measuring (resilience in first-year teachers, loneliness in graduate students, perceived stress in oncology nurses). Two to four validated scales come back with the scale name, the construct, the recommended citation, the typical sample size, and a one-sentence rationale. When the recommendation matches ReliCheck's built-in library, one click stands up a starter survey.

Lives in the Questions builder toolbar.

↻

The assists chain together

You can run any of these in isolation, but they compound.

A common path: start with Validated Scale Match to anchor on something proven, use Item Drafter for the gaps, run Construct Map to group what you have, and finish with Purpose Alignment against the actual decision. Item Audit runs on every edit, so the wording check is always on.

Suggested order: Validated Scale Match, Item Drafter, Construct Map, Purpose Alignment.
