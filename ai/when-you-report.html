Reporting tools

Four assists, one page.

★

Results Verdict

Know what these results can support before you put your name on them.

A four-tier verdict sits directly under the Strength Index ring: yes, yes with cautions, use with care, or not yet. Three buckets break it down further (safe to use for, use caution for, not recommended for), and every caution references the actual number it's built on so a reviewer can audit the call without leaving the page.

Top of the Strength Index tab.

◎

Response Quality Audit

Find out whether the response set itself is clean before you report on it.

Three deterministic checks run on the dataset: straight-lining, duplicate response vectors, and very short open-ended answers. The Audit reports severity in plain language and recommends whether to review, recollect, or proceed.

Strength Index tab, under the verdict card.

📝

Test Analytics Brief

Read your classroom test the way a department chair would.

The paragraph names the strongest and weakest items by their label, flags possibly miskeyed questions before they cost another semester, and reports whether the test reliably ranked the students. Plain language, no jargon, ready to forward. Under the surface: reliability, pass rate, item difficulty, and item quality.

Top of every test analysis dashboard.

✎

Report Drafter

Get a starting paragraph in the voice that matches your audience.

One click on the Strength Index turns the live snapshot into a two-to-three paragraph draft you can edit. Researcher voice writes a Methods-and-Results paragraph with alpha, omega, factor count, and cumulative variance cited precisely. HR-lead voice writes a leadership summary with the headline first and the numbers in parentheses. Teacher voice writes a department-friendly paragraph with a specific next step. Numbers come from the snapshot only, never invented.

Top of the Strength Index tab.
