How it works

Conversational Take

Let respondents answer in their own words.

The survey opens as a chat instead of a form. Each question appears in plain language; the respondent replies naturally ("agree," "5," "I love this"); the answer gets recorded against the right option and confirmed back in one sentence before the next question. Same data, same reports, just an easier way to answer.

↧

Why it lifts response rates

Long surveys lose respondents to form fatigue.

A chat reads faster, feels less like work, and gives respondents room to answer with shades of meaning a radio button can't capture ("I love this part but the workload is brutal"). The structured value gets pulled out and confirmed back, so the respondent can correct it if needed. Same data, lower abandon rate, better accessibility for people who struggle with dense form layouts (including screen readers and assistive tech).

Designed with assistive-tech use in mind.

🛡

Built-in cost and abuse guardrails

You don't have to worry about one noisy respondent running up the AI bill.

The endpoint behind the chat is rate-limited to 240 calls per hour per IP. Each call uses a 400-token output budget and a prompt constrained to the question shape, and server-side validation clamps Likert values to the survey's scale and rejects out-of-range single-choice indices. An off-topic reply can't poison the dataset.

Per-IP rate limit, validated output, no respondent identifiers passed to the model.
