# Methodology Briefs

**Where it lives:** CFA, Measurement Invariance, IRT, Mediation, Moderation, and Multilevel Model cards.

**Outcome.** Get a plain-language read of the methods accreditation reviewers expect to see.

Each method has its own short paragraph: structure, group consistency, item behavior, indirect effects, moderation regions, and grouped-data variance.

**More detail.**

- **Confirmatory factor analysis** reads as whether the survey structure holds up. Under the surface: CFI, TLI, RMSEA, SRMR.
- **Measurement invariance** answers whether the survey works the same way across groups.
- **Item response theory** translates discrimination and item information into "this item separates higher and lower scorers" language. Under the surface: Graded Response, 2PL, 3PL, and two-dimensional MIRT, including which items cross-load on two traits.
- **Mediation** reports the indirect effect with bootstrap confidence. Under the surface: BCa intervals, log-odds scale for binary outcomes.
- **Moderation** reports where the effect is real and where it isn't. Under the surface: the Johnson-Neyman region.
- **Multilevel models** answer whether the grouping (classrooms, teams, sites) matters. Under the surface: ICC and variance components for two-level linear, two-level logistic GLMM, and three-level linear models.
