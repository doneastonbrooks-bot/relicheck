Employee Engagement 2026: Executive Summary

Q1 pulse, 412 of 487 employees responded (84%). Prepared for the HR Leadership Team.

"Engagement is steady. Recognition is the one place that needs a focused fix this quarter."

The Q1 engagement pulse is in. Response rate held at 84%, slightly above last year, and the data quality checks all came back clean. Three of the four scales we track are in a healthy range, and one scale, Recognition, is the place we need to focus before the next pulse.

Headline findings

  • Engagement is steady. Average score 4.10 on a 5-point scale, with no meaningful change since Q4. The construct holds together cleanly (α = 0.87).
  • Recognition has two weak items. Items R1 ("My manager recognizes my work") and R2 ("Recognition is part of our culture") show low item-total correlations (0.18 and 0.21). Removing or rewriting them lifts the scale's reliability from 0.78 to about 0.84.
  • Growth scores diverge by tenure. Employees in their first two years rated Growth 0.45 points higher than the 5+ year cohort (Cohen's d = 0.58, 95% CI [0.34, 0.82]).
  • Intent to stay is strong overall. Average 4.20, with no subgroup falling below 3.80. Engineering and Sales sit highest; Operations sits at the bottom of the range.

Reliability check

Cronbach's alpha for the overall scale was 0.84 (95% CI [0.81, 0.86]) with McDonald's omega at 0.87. The factor structure replicates the three-factor model used in the Q4 pulse, with each item loading on its intended subscale.

Subgroup highlights

Department-level scores were calculated wherever the responding group met our privacy floor (n >= 10). Two groups in Finance fell below this threshold and were rolled up into a parent category.

What employees said

From 203 open-ended responses, the AI theming pass surfaced three top themes: "manager recognition" (mentioned in 38% of responses), "career path clarity" (29%), and "workload balance" (24%). Sentiment was net positive across all three.

Methods

Survey administered between Mar 14 and Apr 4, 2026. Items used a 5-point Likert scale (Strongly disagree to Strongly agree). Reliability statistics computed in ReliCheck using Cronbach's alpha and McDonald's omega. Subgroup contrasts use Welch's t-test with Cohen's d effect size; multi-group contrasts use one-way ANOVA with eta-squared.

Recommended next steps

  • Rewrite items R1 and R2 before the Q2 pulse. Pilot with 30 employees first.
  • Brief department leaders on Growth differences by tenure, especially in Operations.
  • Re-run subgroup analysis on Finance once headcount stabilizes.
  • Repeat this report for the Q2 pulse using the same structure for easy comparison.
