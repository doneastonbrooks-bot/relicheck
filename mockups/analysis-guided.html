1 OF 3 FINDINGS
1
What ReliCheck found

Two questions on the Recognition scale are weakening your survey's consistency.

Items 4 and 9 do not move together with the rest of the Recognition scale. The scale would be more reliable if these two items were revised or removed.

2
Why this matters

Inconsistent items add noise to every score you report.

When two items pull in different directions than the rest of the scale, the total score becomes harder to interpret. Two employees with the same true level of recognition could end up with different scores because of how those items were phrased, not because their experiences differ.

3
What to do next

Three options, ranked by impact.

  • Rewrite Item 4: "My manager recognizes my work" is double-barreled with the next pulse's wording on feedback frequency. Split into two cleaner statements.
  • Drop Item 9: "Recognition is part of our culture" reads as a culture item, not a personal-experience item. Consider moving it to the Culture scale.
  • Run a pilot with the revised items on 30 employees before the next full pulse.
