Quan Analysis — where to start

i Interpretation

Your project has 6 Likert items and 150 responses. Strong enough for reliability, validity, group comparisons, and Pearson correlations. Multivariate models (regression, IRT) need more responses.

! Limitations

n=150 keeps confidence intervals wide on subgroup analyses. Predictor models stay out of reach until n ≥ 200.

→ Recommended next step

Start with Reliability. In an Explanatory design the numbers come first; the scale's internal consistency is the foundation of every claim that follows.

✓ Methods text

Quantitative analyses were conducted on a 6-item Likert scale completed by 150 respondents. Internal consistency, factor structure, and group comparisons were tested in the order outlined in the Explanatory sequence.

Reliability — what to know

i Interpretation

The scale shows good internal consistency. Cronbach's α = 0.82 indicates items measure the same underlying construct.

! Limitations

Two items (INC3, INC6) have item-total correlations below 0.30. Their removal would raise α to ~0.85, suggesting they may not belong to this construct.

→ Recommended next step

Review the flagged items. Consider rewording or removing. Re-run reliability after the revision before moving to group comparisons.

✓ Methods text

The Inclusion scale demonstrated good internal consistency, Cronbach's α = 0.824 (95% CI [0.78, 0.86]) and McDonald's ω = 0.847 (N = 150, 6 items). Two items (INC3, INC6) showed item-total correlations below 0.30 and are flagged for review.
