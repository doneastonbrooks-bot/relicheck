### What's new since the last preview

A lot has shifted in the last couple of weeks. The Studio now uses a three-column shell — workflow sidebar on the left, your work in the middle, methodology guidance on the right — with a few new objects worth knowing about:

- A **Strength Index ring** in the top-right corner of every workspace, showing the credibility score for your project at a glance. Click it for the full per-domain breakdown.
- A **trust journey strip** below the workspace title (Questions › Analysis › Interpretation › Decision) — tells you which trust stage the current workspace contributes to.
- A new **"Your outputs"** workspace under *Defend the Report* — a single place to download the report, the coded variables dataset, the joint display, and the matrix. Replaces the old hunt across multiple tabs.
- An optional **"Express mode"** on the new-project screen if you want to skip the framing wizard and start analyzing immediately.
- An **`?v3=0` escape hatch** appended to any URL drops you back to the older legacy shell if something in the new one blocks your work.

### What I would like you to try

The workspace is still a six-stop journey. Please walk through all six in order:

1. **Set Up** — confirm the project title and description read clearly.
2. **Structure Data** — upload one of your own datasets (CSV or Excel works), or use Express mode if you'd rather pick a design first and bring data in later.
3. **Choose Design** — pick one of the three designs (Explanatory, Exploratory, or Convergent). I'm especially curious whether the design descriptions are enough for you to choose with confidence.
4. **Analyze** — this is where the bulk of the work happens. Spend real time on the **Codebook & Themes** workspace. The three panels (theme list on the left, codebook entry form in the middle, evidence drawer on the right) are new and we want to know whether they hold up under actual coding work.
5. **Integrate Evidence** — open the Joint Display and the Integration paragraphs.
6. **Defend the Report** — open the new **Your outputs** workspace, generate a draft report, and try the Word **and** PDF exports. Please open the Word file in Word, Google Docs, **and** Pages — I want to know it renders correctly in all three.

### What I am listening for

Anything that feels off. Friction, confusing language, a button that does not behave the way you expect, a missing affordance, a moment where you had to guess. Praise is welcome too, but the rough edges are what I need most.

A few specific questions, if any of them resonate:

- Did the six-stop strip and the trust-journey strip make the workflow legible, or did one or both feel like one more thing on the screen?
- Did the Codebook entry form give you what a dissertation-grade codebook needs, or are fields missing?
- Were the design descriptions on Choose Design enough, or did you want more guidance?
- The Word and PDF exports now render real native Word documents (not HTML wrappers). When you open the file in Word vs Google Docs vs Pages — does it look right in all three? Does the report read like a research deliverable or a status page?
- The Strength Index ring shows your credibility score in the workspace head and the report. Does the per-domain "What the score is telling you" column actually tell you what to do next, or is it noise?
