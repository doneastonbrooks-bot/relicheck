Thanks again for agreeing to take an early look at the ReliCheck Mixed Methods Studio. The closed beta opens on June 1, and your feedback before then will shape what the first cohort of researchers sees.

### How to get in

1. Go to https://relichecksurvey.com/login.html
2. Sign in with **[Your Login Email]**. If you have not set a password, use the reset link on the login page.
3. Once you land in the app, open the **Mixed Methods** workspace from the left sidebar and select **[Sample Project Name]**.

### What I would like you to try

The workspace is now a six-stop journey. Please walk through all six in order:

1. **Set Up** — confirm the project title and description read clearly.
2. **Structure Data** — review the data, framing, and roles summary.
3. **Choose Design** — pick one of the three designs (Explanatory, Exploratory, or Convergent) and complete the Confirm sub-step. I am especially curious whether the design descriptions are enough for you to choose with confidence.
4. **Analyze** — this is where the bulk of the work happens. Inside Analyze, please spend real time on the **Codebook & Themes** workspace. The three panels (theme list on the left, codebook entry form in the middle, evidence drawer on the right) are new and we want to know whether they hold up under actual coding work.
5. **Integrate Evidence** — open the Joint Display and the Integration paragraphs.
6. **Defend the Report** — generate a draft report and try a DOCX or PDF export.

### What I am listening for

Anything that feels off. Friction, confusing language, a button that does not behave the way you expect, a missing affordance, a moment where you had to guess. Praise is welcome too, but the rough edges are what I need most.

A few specific questions, if any of them resonate:

- Did the six-stop strip make the workflow legible, or did it feel like one more thing on the screen?
- Did the Codebook entry form give you what a dissertation-grade codebook needs, or are fields missing?
- Were the design descriptions on Choose Design enough, or did you want more guidance?

### How to send notes back

Just reply to this email. Bullet points, screenshots, voice notes, whatever is easiest. No structure required, no deadline. The sooner I see your notes the more of them I can act on before launch, but I would rather have careful feedback later than rushed feedback now.

If anything breaks outright, send me the URL and a quick description and I will dig in.

Thanks for doing this. Researchers like you are the reason the tool exists, and your read on the current build matters.
