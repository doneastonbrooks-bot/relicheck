Pre-Publish Check

Catch the issues that hurt your survey before it goes live.

Reliability, factor structure, and response quality come from one place: the survey itself. The ReliCheck Pre-Publish Check reviews every item for purpose alignment, scale balance, wording, accessibility, and respondent experience, then returns a go / no-go verdict with item-level fixes you can apply in one click.

Updated May 2026 · Reading time: 8 min · Audience: research, evaluation, HR, instructors

A pre-flight check, run by the same engine that scores your results.

Most survey problems are baked in before the first response arrives. A scale that mixes positive and negative wording without a reverse-scoring plan, a construct with only two items, an open-ended prompt placed before the rating items it depends on: any one of these caps the quality of the data you collect.

The Pre-Publish Check runs eight automated reviews on the survey draft, returns a single verdict (Go, Go with cautions, Revise, Block), and lists the specific items that triggered each warning. Most issues come with a one-click rewrite suggestion from the AI item assistant.

Verdict tiers

Four bands. Each one tells you what to do next.

Go
Ready to publish
All eight checks pass. Hit publish whenever you are ready.
Go with cautions
Minor fixes optional
Cosmetic warnings only. Publish, or apply suggested rewrites in a minute.
Revise
Fix before launch
One or more checks failed. Open the item assistant and apply suggestions.
Block
Critical issues
A construct has zero items, accessibility is failing, or a required field is missing.

A Block verdict prevents accidental publication of a survey that cannot be analyzed. Go with cautions is the most common verdict on first run.

The eight checks

What runs on every draft.

Each check has a specific psychometric or respondent-experience justification. The intent is to flag the issues that show up later as low alpha, weak factor structure, or response-quality warnings on the analytics dashboard.

1

Purpose alignment

Reads the stated survey purpose and tags each item as core, supporting, tangential, or off-topic. Suggests items to remove and gaps to fill.

  • Items that drift from the stated goal
  • Constructs the purpose names but the items miss
2

Construct coverage

Counts items per construct. Two-item constructs limit reliability estimation; one-item constructs cannot be analyzed at all.

  • Constructs with fewer than 3 items
  • Untagged items that look like they belong to a construct
3

Scale balance

Checks Likert items for a mix of positively and negatively keyed wording, and confirms reverse-scoring is set where intended.

  • All-positive scales (acquiescence risk)
  • Reverse-worded items without a reverse-score flag
4

Item wording

Flags double-barreled, leading, vague, or jargon-heavy items. Each flag comes with a one-click AI rewrite suggestion.

  • Two ideas in one item ("clear and engaging")
  • Loaded language ("how disappointed are you")
5

Reading level

Computes Flesch-Kincaid grade level per item. Default target is grade 8; you can set a different target for specialist audiences.

  • Items above the target reading level
  • Acronyms and unexplained terms
6

Order & flow

Reviews the question sequence for rapport, sensitive-topic placement, and dependencies between items.

  • Sensitive demographics placed first
  • Open-ended items placed before the rating items they depend on
7

Accessibility

Checks the rendered survey against WCAG 2.1 AA. Confirms labels, color contrast, keyboard navigation, and screen-reader announcements.

  • Color-only state indicators
  • Inputs without programmatic labels
8

Length & respondent burden

Estimates completion time from item count and item type. Flags surveys likely to drop respondents partway through.

  • Estimated time over the target threshold
  • Long matrix grids that fatigue respondents
How it runs

Three steps. Roughly thirty seconds.

1

Open your draft

Click Pre-Publish Check from the survey builder toolbar, or run it from the Distribute view before sending invitations. Drafts of any size are accepted.

2

Review the verdict

The verdict pill appears within seconds. Each failing check links to the specific items that triggered the warning, with a plain-language explanation.

3

Apply fixes inline

Most warnings come with a one-click AI rewrite. Accept, edit, or dismiss each suggestion. Re-run the check until you reach Go.

Why this matters

Pre-launch fixes are cheap. Post-launch fixes break your data.

A survey item that is double-barreled, leading, or off-topic does not become defensible after responses are collected. Edits made mid-administration split the dataset into incompatible versions; edits made after close of fielding mean the original respondents answered a different question than the one being reported on.

The Pre-Publish Check exists because the cheapest moment to fix a survey is the moment before it ships. A two-minute rewrite of an ambiguous item is the difference between an alpha of 0.62 and an alpha of 0.81 on the same construct three weeks later.

The check is not a substitute for human review. An expert reviewer brings construct knowledge, audience knowledge, and political judgment that automated checks cannot. The Pre-Publish Check handles the mechanical pass so reviewers can spend their attention on the questions that actually require it.

Where it sits in the workflow

Pre-Publish Check runs at draft time. The ReliCheck Survey Strength Index™ runs after responses arrive. The two are paired: Pre-Publish Check optimizes the instrument before launch; the Strength Index scores the data after launch. A Go verdict on Pre-Publish Check is the strongest single predictor of a Strong-or-better SSI™ once responses are in.

Common questions

What the check does, and what it does not do.

Does Pre-Publish Check work on imported surveys?

Yes. Surveys imported from Qualtrics .qsf files, pasted text, or any of the ReliCheck templates are eligible. The check runs on the parsed item structure, so the import path does not matter.

Will the AI rewrite suggestions change my items without my approval?

No. Every suggestion is a proposal. You see the original wording and the suggested rewrite side by side, and choose Accept, Edit, or Dismiss. Nothing changes until you click Accept.

Can I publish with a Block verdict?

Block is the only verdict that prevents publication, and it triggers only on issues that make the data unanalyzable: zero items in a tagged construct, missing required fields, or accessibility failures that violate WCAG 2.1 AA. Fix the underlying issue and re-run; the verdict updates automatically.

How does Pre-Publish Check relate to the Survey Strength Index?

Pre-Publish Check is pre-launch. The ReliCheck Survey Strength Index™ is post-launch. The check optimizes the instrument before responses arrive; the Strength Index scores the data after responses arrive. Surveys that ship with a Go verdict reliably produce stronger Strength Index scores.

Can I customize the reading-level target or the time-burden threshold?

Yes. Both are settings on each survey. Default reading-level target is grade 8; default time-burden threshold is 8 minutes. For specialist audiences (graduate research, technical staff) you can raise the reading-level target without affecting other surveys.

Does the check store my draft items?

The check runs on the same draft already saved to your account. It does not transmit your items to any third party. AI rewrite suggestions are generated under the same data-handling terms as the rest of ReliCheck's AI features.

How long does it take?

Roughly thirty seconds for a typical 20-item survey. Longer surveys (100+ items) finish in under two minutes. The check runs asynchronously, so you can keep editing while it works.
